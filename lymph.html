The Lymphatic System

The lymphatic system is a network of tissues and organs that helps to remove waste and toxins from the body. It also helps to fight infection and disease.

The lymphatic system is made up of the following components:

  • Lymph nodes
  • Lymph vessels
  • Lymph

Lymph nodes are small, bean-shaped organs that are located throughout the body. They filter lymph and remove waste products and toxins.

Lymph vessels are thin tubes that carry lymph throughout the body. They connect lymph nodes to each other and to the bloodstream.

Lymph is a clear fluid that contains white blood cells and other immune cells. It helps to fight infection and disease.

Functions of the Lymphatic System

The lymphatic system has a number of important functions, including:

  • Removing waste products and toxins from the body
  • Fighting infection and disease
  • Transporting nutrients and oxygen to cells
  • Regulating fluid balance in the body

Common Lymphatic System Disorders

There are a number of disorders that can affect the lymphatic system, including:

  • Lymphedema
  • Lymphoma
  • Leukemia

Lymphedema is a condition that causes swelling in the arms, legs, or other parts of the body. It is caused by a blockage in the lymphatic system.

Lymphoma is a type of cancer that starts in the lymphatic system. It can affect lymph nodes, lymph vessels, and other organs.

Leukemia is a type of cancer that starts in the blood-forming tissues of the body. It can also affect the lymphatic system.
